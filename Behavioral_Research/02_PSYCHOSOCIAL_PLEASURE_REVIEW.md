# 02_PSYCHOSOCIAL_PLEASURE_REVIEW: THE SCOPING AUDIT
**SOURCE:** Reis, J., et al. (2021)
**STATUS:** SYSTEMATIC SYNTHESIS [v8.5]

## I. SYSTEMIC FACTORS OF PLEASURE
The "Pleasure Gap" is driven by gendered scripts, power imbalances, and the "Coital Imperative."

### 1. INDIVIDUAL ENABLERS
*   **Sexual Autonomy:** The feeling that behaviors are self-determined. When undermined, pleasure crashes.
*   **Sexual Assertiveness:** The ability to communicate desires and negotiate boundaries.
*   **Arousability:** A stronger predictor of pleasure in women than in men.
*   **Body Appreciation:** Positively correlated with entitlement to pleasure and frequency of orgasm.

### 2. INTERPERSONAL CONSTRAINTS
*   **Sexual Compliance:** Willing consent to unwanted sex (common in young women/casual sex). Promotes partner's pleasure at the cost of one's own.
*   **The Burden of Responsibility:** Women often feel responsible for both their own and their partner's pleasure, leading to cognitive overload.
*   **Feigning (The Faked Orgasm):** Used strategically to bolster partner's ego or terminate unpleasurable encounters.

## II. BEHAVIORAL CORRELATES
*   **Cunnilingus:** Central to pleasure for many, yet often "taken for granted" or negotiated as a trade.
*   **Anal Sex:** Motivated by pleasure for some, but often associated with coercion or "compliance" in others.
*   **Masturbation:** Correlated with higher self-efficacy and empowerment; frequency predicts pleasure during intercourse.
*   **Contraception:** Condoms and hormonal methods often perceived as "Pleasure Killers" (Tactile/Hormonal interference).

## III. THE AGE/EXPERIENCE CURVE
*   **Learning Curve:** Pleasure tends to increase with age and partner familiarity/intimacy.
*   **The "Silent" Gap:** Older women (55+) face societal silence regarding their desire for pleasure, entrenching the "Assexual Elder" myth.
