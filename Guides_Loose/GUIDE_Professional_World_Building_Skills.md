# GUIDE: Professional World Building Skills

## 1. THE CORE TRAITS (THE HARDWARE)

- **Systems-Level Thinking:** Seeing the "matrix" of cause and effect. Every element (e.g., an energy source) must disrupt the economy, military tactics, and religion. Everything is an ecosystem.
- **Forensic Rigor:** A violent allergy to the "Deus Ex Machina". Audit your own logic gates and hold yourself to the established rules of physics and magic.
- **Somatic Awareness:** Understanding that a world is felt before it is understood. Conceptualize how an environment impacts the nervous system (e.g., air tasting of ozone, oppressive gravity).
- **Comfort with Scarcity:** Engineer deliberate friction. Resource scarcity (money, magic, oxygen, time) is the engine of narrative tension.

## 2. THE TECHNICAL SKILLS (THE SOFTWARE)

- **Constraint Engineering:** Define the "costs" of power. Power without biological or physical consequence is invalid geometry. If reality is bent, something must break.
- **Spatial and Zonal Logic:** Design environments that dictate power dynamics and force characters to change their kinetic posture and linguistic style.
- **Macro-Economics & Geopolitics:** Understand resource control and transportation. Conflict is always found in the supply chain.
- **Sociolinguistics:** Build distinct dialects and vocabulary whitelists based on a culture's history (e.g., tactical metaphors for war cultures).

## 3. THE EXECUTION (THE RENDER)

- **The "Iceberg" Methodology:** Write 100 pages of lore, physics, and history for the World Bible, even if the audience only sees 10. The unseen 90% provides the weight and consistency.
- **Trauma-Informed Environmental Design:** The world itself can be the antagonist. Build environments that trigger psychological "brakes" (inhibitions) or "accelerators" (desires).
