# GUIDE: Isometric Metropolis Design Prompts

## 1. CORE CONFIGURATION

- **View & Core Style:** Use "Isometric floor plan" or "isometric architectural illustration".
- **Projection:** Specify "orthographic projection" to ensure spatial logic.
- **Scale:** Define the scale as "large scale, detailed, multi-layered".
- **Core Subject:** Describe the overall estate or metropolis (e.g., "futuristic estate under a single structure", "sprawling interconnected city districts").

## 2. SOMATIC ZONING & DISTINCT AREAS

This is the critical data insertion point. Define each area and its visual data using descriptive lore-based vocabulary.

**Example Definitions:**
- **Gilded Pit:** Rugged, central subterranean market hub, makeshift stalls, contrasting market-dwellers.
- **Upper Residential:** Spiring structures, integrated parks, clean design, bright light.
- **Industrial Sector:** Heavy machinery, exposed gears, busy worker activity, deep shadows.
- **Commercial Center:** Dense shops, neon signs, varying building heights.
- **Nature Reserves:** Winding paths, green spaces.

## 3. NARRATIVE DETAILS & INFRASTRUCTURE

Specify small objects and interactive elements to ground the realism:
- **Circulation:** Detailed pathways, transit lines, and visible infrastructure connections.
- **Life:** Tiny figures interacting, market stalls.
- **Mechanics:** Visible machinery and exposed gears.

## 4. LIGHTING, MOOD, & AESTHETICS

- **Atmosphere:**
    - **Moody Noir:** Long shadows with contrasting bright light from specific nodes.
    - **Futuristic Clean:** Brightly lit with glowing signs and deep pockets of darkness.
- **Medium/Aesthetics:**
    - **Hand-Drawn & Annotated:** Detailed illustration with clear lines and borders.
    - **Stylized Digital Map:** Clean render with clear colors and texture.
    - **Blueprint Style:** Tactical view with glowing lines.
    - **Watercolor & Ink:** Melancholic, abandoned feel.
