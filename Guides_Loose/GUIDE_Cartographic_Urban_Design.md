# GUIDE: Cartographic Urban Design and Construction Management

## 1. STRATEGIC CARTOGRAPHY

Cartography in Project Eidolon is the deliberate manipulation of the world to manage spatial tension and facilitate "narrative osmosis"—where users assimilate complex lore through exploration.

**Core Concepts:**
- **Prospect vs. Refuge:** Balance "Prospect" spaces (open, brightly lit, high vulnerability) with "Refuge" spaces (shielded, enclosed, safe).
- **Narrative Stratigraphy:** Treat environmental assets as archaeological artifacts. Arrange props (e.g., barricaded shopfronts, worn signs) to implicitly tell the history of a zone.

## 2. PROCEDURAL ZONING & COLOR PALETTE

Use standard color-coding for procedural zoning to ensure instant legibility:
- **Residential:** Yellow (Seeks waterfronts/views, avoids industry).
- **Commercial:** Red (Clusters around primary roads and high-density areas).
- **Industrial:** Blue (Typically isolated).
- **Recreational/Parks:** Light Green (Integrated into flood plains or rugged terrain).

## 3. ENVIRONMENTAL VISUALIZATION

Decompose 3D scenes for environmental mapping:
- **Heightmap:** Defines terrain elevation.
- **Splatmap:** Controls terrain texture and color (e.g., grass textures dictating placement of scatter assets like rocks).
- **Thematic Overlays:** Use "Magma Maps" or "Scarlet Mapping" to differentiate safe zones from hazardous phenomena.

## 4. ISOMETRIC CONSTRUCTION LOGIC

Isometric projection communicates height and complex internal structures without ambiguity.

**Construction Rules:**
- **Verticality Chunking:** Limit environments to three functional planes (bottom, middle, top) to prevent cognitive overload.
- **Flow Weight:** Downward flow (one-way drops) is "heavier" and easier to navigate than upward flow.
- **Depth Sorting:** Objects higher up are rendered first; lower objects are rendered last.

## 5. ISOMETRIC LIGHTING PROTOCOL (VOLUME DEFINITION)

- **Main Wall (Lit):** Unshaded base color.
- **Side Wall (Unlit):** Darkened to create depth.
- **Top Wall/Floor (Highlight):** Lightened to simulate a downward light source.

## 6. SPATIAL TENSION CALCULATIONS

Synthesize spatial data to generate "Spatial Tension Curves" based on:
- **Density:** Object compactness.
- **Size:** Spatial dimensions.
- **Openness:** Number of access points.
