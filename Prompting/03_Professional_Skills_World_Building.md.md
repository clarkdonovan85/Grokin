I. The Core Traits (The Hardware)
Systems-Level Thinking:
The ability to see the "matrix" of cause and effect. An expert doesn't just invent a glowing blue energy source; they immediately calculate how that energy source disrupts the local economy, changes military tactics, and alters the religious beliefs of the ruling class. Everything is an ecosystem.
Forensic Rigor (The "Lore Purist"):
A violent allergy to the "Deus Ex Machina." You must possess the discipline to audit your own logic gates. If you establish a rule of physics or magic on day one, you must hold yourself and your characters to that rule on day one hundred, even if it makes writing the narrative harder.
Somatic Awareness (Architectural Empathy):
Understanding that a world is felt before it is understood. This is the ability to conceptualize how an environment impacts the nervous system. Does the air taste like ozone and static? Is the gravity oppressive? You must understand the thermodynamics and sensory reality of a space to make it feel visceral.
The Comfort with Scarcity:
A weak builder gives their characters everything they need. An expert builder engineers deliberate friction. You must understand that resource scarcity—whether it's money, magic, oxygen, or time—is the engine that drives all narrative tension.
II. The Technical Skills (The Software)
Constraint Engineering (The Physics of Magic/Tech):
The most important part of a magic or tech system isn't what it
do; it’s what it
do. You must be able to define the "costs" of power. If a character bends reality, what breaks? Does it cost them their memories? Does it generate a localized thermal runaway? Power without biological or physical consequence is just bad geometry.
Spatial and Zonal Logic:
The ability to design environments that dictate power dynamics. You need the skill to map out distinct zones—like a sterile, glass-walled Boardroom versus a neon-lit, humid subterranean dive bar—and understand how the physical architecture forces characters to change their kinetic posture and linguistic style.
Macro-Economics and Geopolitics:
You don't need a PhD in economics, but you need to know who controls the resources and how they transport them. If the elites live in the "Gilded Spire," who is working in the flooded lower levels to keep their lights on? The conflict is always in the supply chain.
Sociolinguistics (The Cultural Cipher):
The ability to build distinct dialects and vocabulary whitelists based on a culture's history. A civilization built on war will use tactical, combative metaphors for everyday life; a society built on trade will use transactional language.
III. The Execution (The Render)
The "Iceberg" Methodology:
You must write 100 pages of lore, physics, and history for your World Bible, knowing that the audience will only ever see 10 pages of it. The unseen 90% is the mass that gives the visible 10% its devastating weight and consistency.
Trauma-Informed Environmental Design:
Understanding that the world itself can be the antagonist. You must be able to build environments that specifically trigger the psychological "brakes" (inhibitions) or "accelerators" (desires) of the people walking through them.
